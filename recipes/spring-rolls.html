Spring Rolls Recipe

Photo by dinnerthendessert.com

Description

Quick and easy spring rolls! A traditional chinese New Year's recipe.
Best served hot with sweet and sour or spicy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • Spring roll wrappers: 8 sheets
  • Cabbage: 10,5oz
  • Carrots: 2oz
  • White onion: 1,8oz
  • Rice wine: 1oz
  • Peanut oil: as needed
  • Fine salt: to taste
  • White pepper (or black pepper): to taste
  • Egg whites: as needed
  • Peanut oil: as needed

Steps:

  1. To make the spring rolls, first thaw the ready-made wrappers and keep them covered with a slightly damp kitchen towel to prevent them from drying out. Clean and finely slice the cabbage, onion, and carrots into thin strips.

  2. Heat a wok over high heat, then add the vegetable oil and onions. Sauté for a couple of minutes, then add the carrots and cabbage. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Add the rice wine and stir-fry the vegetables for 5 minutes until they are cooked but still crisp. Transfer them to a colander to drain any excess liquid, then spread them out slightly with chopsticks to help preserve their color and texture.

  4. To assemble the rolls, place one wrapper at a time on the towel with one corner facing you. Add a handful of filling to the lower half. Fold the bottom corner up over the filling and roll without pressing too tightly, then fold the side corners toward the center.

  5. Roll tightly from bottom to top and seal the edge with a little egg white, using your fingers or a brush. Be careful not to press too hard or the wrapper may tear. Repeat until all rolls are formed.

  6. Heat the wok again and add plenty of oil, bringing it to about 350°F. Once hot, lower the heat slightly and fry a few rolls at a time, turning them on both sides.

  7. When golden brown on both sides, remove the spring rolls and drain them on paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve hot with sweet and sour or spicy sauce.

Enjoy!
