Carbonara Recipe

Photo by giallozafferano.com

Description

Perfect original recipe for a Pasta Carbonara.
So rich and creamy you will feel like you are eating in a restaurant in Rome!

Ingredients:

  • Pasta(spaghetti, mezze maniche, or bucatini): 14oz
  • Guanciale: 10oz
  • Pecorino Romano cheese: 7oz
  • Egg yolks: 5
  • Black pepper: to taste

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Use only about half the
    usual amount, since Pecorino cheese is already quite salty. Once boiling, cook your
    chosen pasta: long pasta like spaghetti or bucatini works well, but mezze maniche
    are also a great option.

  2. Take the guanciale, remove the rind, and cut it into fairly thick strips, about one
    fourth of an inch wide.

  3. Cook it in a pan over medium heat until the fat becomes translucent. There’s no
    need to add oil, as it will render its own fat. Pour off the rendered fat into a small
    bowl. Return the guanciale to the pan and cook for a few more minutes until crispy,
    then remove from the heat and set aside.

  4. Place the egg yolks in a bowl, add the Pecorino (reserving about two tablespoons
    for garnish), and a generous grind of freshly ground black pepper. Mix briefly with a
    spatula.

  5. Add two spoonfuls of the rendered guanciale fat to the mixture to make it creamy,
    thick, and smooth, stirring well.

  6. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta al dente.

  7. Add the pasta to the pan used for the guanciale (off the heat), then add the egg and
    cheese mixture along with a ladle of pasta water. Mix thoroughly until well
    combined. Add more pasta water if needed. This step must be done off the heat.

  8. When the carbonara becomes creamy (but not runny), thanks to the heat of the
    pasta and the starch in the water, add the guanciale (reserving some for garnish),
    mix briefly, and serve.

  9. Finish with a sprinkle of Pecorino and more black pepper. Your Carbonara is ready
    just like the traditional version served in Roman trattorias!

Enjoy!
